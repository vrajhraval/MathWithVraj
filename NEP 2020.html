The Evolution of Mathematics

From Ancient Tally Marks to Modern Systems & Educational Policies

1. Ancient Foundations (Egypt & Babylonia)

Mathematics began as a practical tool for commerce and agriculture. The Egyptians used geometry to redraw boundaries after the Nile flooded, while the Babylonians developed a base-60 system that we still use today to measure time (60 seconds, 60 minutes).

2. The Greek Influence

The Greeks moved math from "practicality" to "logic." Euclid’s Elements became the standard textbook for 2,000 years. Perhaps the most famous contribution is the Pythagorean Theorem:

\[ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \]

3. The Golden Age of India and the Middle East

During the Middle Ages, Indian mathematicians like Aryabhata and Brahmagupta formalized the concept of Zero. Later, Persian mathematician Al-Khwarizmi wrote the book that gave us the word "Algebra."

\[ e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0 \]

4. National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 Overview

English Medium Details

The NEP 2020 replaces the 34-year-old National Policy on Education (1986). It is designed to change the framework of education by shifting the structure from a rigid 10+2 system to a highly adaptive 5+3+3+4 framework based on cognitive development milestones.

  • Foundational Stage (5 Years): Ages 3-8 (Pre-school to Class 2)
  • Preparatory Stage (3 Years): Ages 8-11 (Classes 3-5)
  • Middle Stage (3 Years): Ages 11-14 (Classes 6-8)
  • Secondary Stage (4 Years): Ages 14-18 (Classes 9-12)
