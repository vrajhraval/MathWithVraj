From Ancient Tally Marks to Modern Calculus

1. Ancient Foundations (Egypt & Babylonia)

Mathematics began as a practical tool for commerce and agriculture. The Egyptians used geometry to redraw boundaries after the Nile flooded, while the Babylonians developed a base-60 system that we still use today to measure time (60 seconds, 60 minutes).

2. The Greek Influence

Perhaps the most famous contribution is the Pythagorean Theorem:

\[ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \]

3. The Golden Age of India and the Middle East

During the Middle Ages, Indian mathematicians like Aryabhata and Brahmagupta formalized the concept of Zero. Later, Persian mathematician Al-Khwarizmi wrote the book that gave us the word "Algebra."

Without the Hindu-Arabic numeral system ($0, 1, 2, 3...$), modern science would be impossible.

4. The Scientific Revolution

In the 17th century, Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz independently developed Calculus. This allowed us to calculate the motion of planets, the flow of fluids, and the complexities of change over time.
