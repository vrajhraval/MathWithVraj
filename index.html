1. Ancient Foundations (Egypt & Babylonia)

Mathematics began as a practical tool for commerce and agriculture. The Egyptians used geometry to redraw boundaries after the Nile flooded, while the Babylonians developed a base-60 system.

2. The Greek Influence

Perhaps the most famous contribution is the Pythagorean Theorem:

\[ a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \]

3. The Golden Age of India and the Middle East

Indian mathematicians like Aryabhata and Brahmagupta formalized the concept of Zero. Without the Hindu-Arabic numeral system ($0, 1, 2, 3...$), modern science would be impossible.

4. The Scientific Revolution

In the 17th century, Isaac Newton and Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz independently developed Calculus.
